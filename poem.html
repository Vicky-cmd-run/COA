COA Poem

A rhythmic journey through Computer Organization

In the heart of silicon, a mind so small, The CPU stands, and it does it all. It fetches commands from memory’s lane, Decodes them fast, then runs the chain. Registers hold the thoughts so tight, A flash of logic, pure and bright. The ALU crunches numbers with flair, While control units lead with utmost care. A clock ticks time in binary beats, Guiding all the digital feats. Cache is swift, a loyal friend, Though RAM awaits around the bend. Buses hum with traffic flow, From I/O devices to memory below. And in the depths of this machine, The architecture stays sleek and clean. From RISC to CISC, instructions fly, Pipelines push them, stacked up high. But hazards lurk in subtle ways— Stalls and bubbles cause delays. Yet through it all, the system sings, Executing loops and logic things. A symphony of tech and art— COA: the computer’s heart.
